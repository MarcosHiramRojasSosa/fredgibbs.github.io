In broad terms, my research interests lie in the confluence of medicine and natural philosophy in the medieval and early modern periods. I'm also interested in the circulation and diffusion of medical knowledge.

Most of my current projects attempt to use new digital research methodologies to provide new historical perspectives through new scales and combinations of historical data.

My poison manuscript presents a new history of premodern toxicology based on how physicians discussed the nature of poison in medical literature from 1200-1600, especially its relationship to medicine, plague, contagion, disease.

Here I post essays about various issues confronting the so-called digital humanities. Sometimes I publish things elsewhere.

At UNM, I teach courses on the digital humanities and on various topics in the history of medicine and science.
